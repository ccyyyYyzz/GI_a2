# 3. Section-by-section execution

## 3.1 Introduction — One hidden channel, two consequences

### Single claim

A bucket measurement is produced by a hidden dynamic channel; treating that channel only as nuisance misses both the scene-information boundary and the possibility of a second measurement.

### Three-paragraph structure

**Paragraph 1 — the paradox, not the background.** Open on scalar compression: a bucket camera keeps one number per pattern while detector live time and medium gain remain hidden. State the two consequences immediately: the hidden state can erase scene information, but its temporal imprint can identify the medium. The phrase **“ghost imaging becomes its own medium monitor”** should land at the end of paragraph 1 as the question/promise, not after a page of setup.

**Paragraph 2 — the boundary.** State that an exact missing-information specialization turns detector-state uncertainty into a measurable scene-information loss and a jitter-capped ridge. Give the two empirical consequences only: global power control and the confirmed imaging/time gains.

**Paragraph 3 — why the second product is logically necessary.** A positive operating law is not yet a boundary, so five preregistered closure tests probe the remaining count-only degrees of freedom. Their joint negative result motivates reusing, rather than correcting away, the hidden medium state. End with the paper’s thesis sentence.

### Opening-sentence strategy

Preferred conceptual opening:

> A bucket camera records one number per pattern, but that number is produced by a dynamic hidden channel.

Second sentence:

> Detector live time and medium fluctuations are usually treated as distortions to be corrected; here they determine both what the scene channel can never recover and what the same record can measure instead.

Do not open with “Ghost imaging has attracted…,” a list of applications, dead-time definitions, DCS history, or a contribution-number list.

### Transition out

> We first quantify the information removed by the hidden state before asking how that boundary should be used.

# 4. The five negatives as one discovery

## 4.1 Editorial reframing

Never call this “five failed methods.” Call it:

> **a preregistered closure experiment for the integrated-count scene channel.**

The scientific question is singular:

> Is the global ridge merely a convenient baseline, or is it the practical optimum of the declared count-only experiment?

The five tests are valuable because they span the remaining controllable axes **after** the detector has compressed each exposure to one count:

- spatial geometry/library;
- measurement allocation;
- temporal order;
- estimator efficiency;
- exact count likelihood.

This is a completeness argument over a declared class, not a chronology of increasingly desperate ideas.

## 4.2 Main-text presentation: exactly three paragraphs

**Paragraph 1 — design of the closure test.** State the common comparator, common resource ledger, preregistered `1 dB` materiality bar, and the division into design-side versus inference-side freedoms.

**Paragraph 2 — design-side closure.** Report in one sentence: geometry/library reachability `+0.68 dB < 1.0`, allocator `−8.3 dB`, schedule optimization `+0.04 dB`. Do not explain the three algorithms here.

**Paragraph 3 — inference-side closure and conclusion.** Report estimator efficiency near `99%` (`<=0.2 dB` headroom) and exact conditional likelihood `+0.41 dB < 1.0`. Conclude with the precise scope:

> Together these tests do not prove a universal optimum over every imaginable optical measurement; they close the declared integrated-count class from both the design and software sides.

Then pivot immediately:

> Once the scalar scene channel is closed, the remaining question is not how to manufacture another fraction of a decibel from the same count, but whether the hidden channel can become a second output.

No paragraph-per-negative structure is permitted.

## 4.3 Figure 4 — the boundary-discovery figure

Title concept: **“Double-sided closure of the count-only scene channel.”**

**Panel a — intervention map.** A left-to-right pipeline from pattern design to count likelihood with the five intervention points placed on it. Enclose all five inside one box labeled **same integrated-count observation**. This visual teaches that the campaigns are orthogonal probes of one class.

**Panel b — common materiality plot.** Plot incremental image-quality gain over the global-ridge baseline on one dB axis, with a clearly shaded `1 dB` materiality region. Use two braces:

- **design side:** `+0.68`, `−8.3`, `+0.04`;
- **inference side:** `<=0.2` headroom, `+0.41`.

Use a capped arrow for the estimator-efficiency upper bound rather than pretending it is an observed gain. If `−8.3 dB` compresses the positive points, use a small broken-axis inset, not a second figure.

**Right-edge visual:** a single arrow leaves the count-only box sideways—not upward in scene dB—and is labeled **second scientific output**. This is the handoff to DLGI.

The five full campaign tables, bank construction, allocator details, ordering protocol, estimator tests, exact-likelihood derivation, bootstrap tables, and all internal acronyms move wholesale to Supplementary Note S6.

## 4.4 MOLT disposition

**Cut MOLT entirely from the main narrative.** It is too interesting to receive one parenthetical sentence and too orthogonal to receive a main section. Including it would turn the closure section back into a graveyard tour and introduce a second meaning of “saturation.”

Place the Masked Laplace–Power-Sum theorem and its three independent image-level kill computations in a self-contained supplementary note titled:

> **Detector-side escape beyond the count-only model: a real operator with insufficient matched-resource image strength.**

The Discussion may contain one sentence pointing to that note as evidence that adding a mathematically new channel is not sufficient when the channel is Fisher-weak. No MOLT acronym, dose table, or null-pair figure in the main paper.

# 5. One record, two scientific outputs

This section appears in full only after all R34 C1–C7 bars pass.

## 5.1 Single claim

When the hidden medium state carries parameters of scientific interest, retaining the raw bucket stream allows joint scene and medium inference; the same canonical confusion modes tax both ledgers, and nuisance-orthogonal scheduling can improve both rather than trading one against the other.

## 5.2 Theory content in the main text

Use the block Fisher matrix once:

```text
I = [A  C; C^T  B].
```

Define the two Schur-complement ledgers and the whitened cross operator `K`. State, without a long proof, that `KK^T` and `K^T K` share the same nonzero eigenvalues, giving equal normalized determinant retention in the two ledgers. This is the **Canonical-Confusion Ledger Reciprocity** result.

State the O4-A consequence plainly:

> Reducing the cross block while preserving the oracle ledgers protects both products; paired scheduling is not a transfer of information from scene to medium.

The full proof, pseudoinverse version, path-versus-hyperparameter distinction, and `(A,B,K)` matrices move to Supplementary Note S7.

## 5.3 Prior-art placement

DCS priority must be handled **before** the DLGI result, in three sentences maximum:

1. DCS/DWS already estimate medium dynamics from dedicated temporal intensity fluctuations.
2. Pilot/interpolated GI monitoring already inserts reference measurements to estimate gain.
3. The claimed distinction is the simultaneous recovery of an unknown scene and quantitative medium hyperparameters from the same ordinary programmable bucket record, with no pilot/reference acquisition and with a joint confusion certificate.

Do not postpone this distinction to a defensive Discussion section. The reader should know what is and is not claimed before seeing the performance plot.

## 5.4 Confirmatory evidence sequence

1. Byte-identical/equal-ledger statement.
2. Model/gauge residual check in one sentence.
3. Pilot-free versus pilot and oracle precision for `t_c` and CV.
4. Calibrated interval coverage and width.
5. Scene noninferiority.
6. Schedule ordering predicted by `(A,B,K)`.
7. One compact edge-domain statement.

Do not report gain-path correlation `0.998` in the main text. It was a seed diagnostic, not a parameter-certification endpoint.

**Panel a — comparator geometry.** Three compact columns:

# 7. Referee pre-emption by architecture

## 7.1 “There is no experiment”

**Architectural answer:** the paper begins with an exact statistical model and theorem, labels every optical schematic as a simulation model, separates theorem/verification/campaign evidence in the figure grammar, and ends with a concrete bench falsification paragraph. The main Results use preregistered held-out cohorts and exact ledger accounting rather than illustrative simulation alone. Methods provide code/data availability and physical parameter mapping.

Do not answer this attack by pretending the simulations are an experiment. State **preregistered numerical campaign** consistently. The final Discussion should specify the minimal rotating-diffuser validation: same pattern stream, one bucket detector, independently controllable decorrelation time, and an external monitor used only as ground truth.

## 7.2 “DLGI is just Kalman smoothing”

**Architectural answer:** the DLGI section opens with the joint Fisher block and reciprocity result, then the equal-ledger pilot/oracle test, then calibrated intervals. Kalman/RTS appears only in Methods as one computational implementation. Figure 5 measures scientific outputs and uncertainty, not path correlation or smoother fit.

A method whose claim disappears when the algorithm name is removed is “just Kalman.” This architecture makes the claim survive algorithm substitution.

## 7.3 “DCS already measures decorrelation time”

**Architectural answer:** Figure 5a and the first three sentences of Section 5 distinguish dedicated medium fluctuation records, pilot-interleaved monitoring, and one ordinary unknown-scene GI record. DCS priority is conceded before performance is shown. The novelty claim is the joint record reuse plus scene/medium confusion certificate, not the measurement of `t_c` itself.

## 7.4 “Simulation-only results cannot support generality”

**Architectural answer:** the DLGI results are organized on dimensionless axes (`T/t_c`, `Delta/t_c`, CV, photon SNR), include both edge failures, require held-out scalar-gain residual tests, and use the phrase **model-certified**. Figure 5 contains the failure domain in the same visual as the success domain. The Discussion states exactly which physical violation—spatially varying transfer rather than scalar gain—kills the model.

Generality comes from dimensionless structure and explicit failure boundaries, not from adjectives.

## 7.5 “Missing-information and orthogonality machinery are classical”

**Architectural answer:** classical lineage is acknowledged in the first theory paragraph, not buried in Discussion. The paper never claims the generic Louis identity as new. The main contribution is the connected optical consequence chain: hidden-state covariance → jitter-capped ridge → confirmed global-control gain → double-sided closure of count-only headroom → reused record with canonical-confusion certification.

The theorem names are subordinate to that chain. Avoid repeated “we derive a new theorem” language.

## 7.6 Additional likely attack: “This is two unrelated papers stitched together”

**Architectural answer:** Figure 1 uses one hidden-state bucket experiment; Section 2 supplies one conditional-score hinge; every section ends by asking the next unavoidable question. The dead-time and medium cases are explicitly two physical specializations of the same statistical architecture, not claimed to be the same bench mechanism. DLGI appears only after the count-only boundary is closed, so it functions as the resolution of the first half rather than an appendix.
