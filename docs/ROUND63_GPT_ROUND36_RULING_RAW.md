# R36 — Slimming ruling: one protagonist, one record, two products

**Overrides:** R35 / issue #27 wherever the two conflict.  
**Binding editorial input:** the R35 four-movement structure is rejected as a program summary. The active manuscript has one protagonist: **DLGI plus the Canonical-Confusion Ledger Reciprocity theorem**.

## Executive ruling

> **Replace `LIMIT → REACH → CLOSE → REUSE` with `DEFINE → DECODE → CERTIFY`.**
>
> The paper first defines the scene and medium as two ledgers of one hidden-state experiment; it then shows how one unchanged bucket record is decoded into the two products; it finally certifies precision, coverage, scene noninferiority, schedule behavior, and the validity domain. Everything else is motivation or lineage, not a movement of the paper.

The manuscript contract is now:

> **The hidden channel that limits bucket imaging is itself an instrument: one unchanged bucket record can reconstruct the scene and measure the medium, while canonical-confusion reciprocity states exactly when the two products remain simultaneously identifiable.**

Hard constraints for the initial Optica submission:

- **at most 7 composed pages including references and back matter;**
- **at most 4 main figures;**
- **no main tables;**
- approximately **80–100 abstract words;**
- no more than **five displayed equations** in the main text;
- no printed “acts,” campaign chronology, internal method tree, or anthology framing.

The ridge law, M1 operating maps, five closure campaigns, MOLT, interval-construction detail, full proofs, and provenance remain complete in Supplementary Notes S1–S10. Demotion is not deletion.

## Section 1 — Introduction

Exactly three paragraphs:

1. **The inversion.** A bucket camera records one number per pattern, but that number is generated by a hidden dynamic channel. The channel is usually treated only as distortion; the paper asks whether the same imprint can serve as a medium measurement.
2. **The boundary paragraph.** Use the frozen four-sentence wording in §2 below. It is the only main-text trace of the five closure campaigns.
3. **The claim and evidence.** State the two-ledger theorem, unchanged acquisition ledger, pilot/oracle baselines, calibrated intervals, scene noninferiority, and validity map. End with the manuscript-contract sentence.

The DCS distinction belongs in one sentence in paragraph 1 or 3: DCS measures medium dynamics from an intensity stream, whereas this paper asks whether a programmable bucket stream containing an unknown scene can deliver **both** products without a dedicated assay channel.

## Section 2 — Two ledgers of one hidden-state experiment

This section is approximately one page and carries the mathematical spine. Keep only:

1. the separated hierarchy `p(y,a|x,theta)=p(y|x,a)p_theta(a)`;
2. the joint observed-information partition
   
   ```text
   I_Y(x,theta) = diag(C_x,C_theta) - E Cov([U_x;U_theta] | Y);
   ```
3. the efficient-ledger Schur complements;
4. the whitened cross operator `K=A^{-1/2}CB^{-1/2}` and the one-line reciprocity statement that both normalized losses have the same nonzero eigenvalues;
5. the O4-A consequence: `C=0` protects both ledgers, not one at the expense of the other.

The abstract Louis/Orchard–Woodbury identity is credited immediately. The novelty claim is the programmable-GI specialization, the canonical-confusion reciprocity, and its schedule/use consequence—not the generic machinery.

**Dead-time sibling disposition:** no M1 number, ridge curve, or `+1.87 dB / 19.13x` callout appears in the initial main text. Supplementary Note S4 preserves that complete sibling instantiation. One cross-reference sentence may be restored only if an editor explicitly asks for evidence that the hidden-information framework extends beyond the gain channel.

# 2. Frozen boundary-summary paragraph

Use the following paragraph **verbatim** in the Introduction. It is four sentences and is the complete main-text representation of the five preregistered certificates:

> Before asking whether the medium can become a second output, we tested whether ordinary bucket imaging still had meaningful scene-recovery headroom. Across five preregistered comparisons, changing the pattern family, redistributing measurements, reordering the same patterns, replacing the reconstruction algorithm, and using the full count statistics produced no material improvement over the global operating rule; one change was strongly harmful. The design-side searches and the inference-side tests therefore bracketed the same conclusion from opposite directions: within the declared count-only experiment, the remaining scene gain is below the threshold fixed in advance. This does not prove optimality over every possible optical measurement, but it changes the next question from how to squeeze another fraction of a decibel from the bucket count to what else the hidden channel has already measured.

No values, method names, internal labels, status codes, or miniature autopsies are added to this paragraph. All numerical evidence remains in Supplementary Note S6.

# 5. Referee pre-emption after slimming

The “two papers stitched together” attack is removed by construction. The new top structural risk is sharper:

> **“This is only a state-space/Kalman estimator evaluated on the same OU simulation that generated its data; the information theorem is decorative.”**

The architecture must answer that objection without a defensive subsection:

1. **Measurement principle before estimator.** Figure 1 and Section 2 define the unchanged optical record, two outputs, gauges, and information geometry before any algorithm is named.
2. **Theorem before implementation.** Reciprocity predicts that suppressing confusion can improve both ledgers and freezes the schedule ordering; Figure 2 tests that prediction independently of reconstruction quality.
3. **Estimator detail is visually demoted.** Filter, smoother, moment estimator, profile likelihood, and calibration code live in the supplement. The main text reports only what statistical product they deliver and whether it is calibrated.
4. **External baselines occupy the result figures.** Pilot, oracle, and plain-linear comparators are embedded in Figures 3–4, so the evidence is not self-referential.
5. **Model falsifiability is part of the claim.** Figure 4 and Section 5 show where the scalar-gain model fails and give a direct bench validation path.

The remaining attacks are handled compactly by placement:

- **DCS priority:** acknowledged in the Introduction and bounded in Discussion, before any “medium monitor” novelty sentence;
- **classical missing-information machinery:** credited in the first theory paragraph;
- **no experiment / simulation-only:** stated in the abstract or Methods capsule and paired with a concrete bench-transfer paragraph, not hidden in limitations;
- **generality:** claimed only over the validated scalar-gain domain, with the failure map in Figure 4.

No separate “referee pre-emption” section appears in the paper.
